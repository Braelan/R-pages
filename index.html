This is a wordcloud of 500 medical wikipedia articles. Why you ask? It gives a visual demonstration of a midway step in building a Naive Bayes classifier that predicts if an article is disease related.

Eventually the classifier was 91% sensitive in predicting article type.

Observations: a) lots of medical terms (suffer, caus, attack, etc) b) the cleaning algorithm didn't catch and magnify suffixes that are often important (in fact it cut them off) and unexpectedly magnified several terms. The data is from a 20n challenge problem.

The full naive Bayes classifier R script is available here.

Directions for this graph are in Chptr. 15 of R in a Nutshell. Here, the cost of treating heart failure is plotted against the number of cases that a given hospital takes per year. Complications and comorbidities are color coded. Costs at some facilities can quadruple the baseline for heart failure once complications and comorbidities come into play. This is a graph of titanic survivors. 0's perished and 1's survived

Newborns to five year olds faired the best, along with 30-35 year olds. Maybe these were families. Passengers in their late teens and twenties faired poorly. Were they in a part of the ship that sunk quickly? or just more selfless? The oldest age group had the worst survival rate.
